The Tenses Blueprint

Master chronological sequencing to spot structural flaws in suspect statements.

Tense Aspect Formula / Structure Strategic Application Example Evidence
Past Simple Subject + Verb 2 (or irregular) Completed physical actions at a designated, fixed point in the past. "The suspect dropped the safe keys at 9:00 PM."
Past Continuous Subject + was/were + Verb-ing Actions that were actively in progress when another event interrupted them. "I was baking bread when the alarm system shut down."
Past Perfect Subject + had + Verb 3 (Past Participle) An action completed strictly *before* another historic event occurred. "By the time the police arrived, the safe cracker had escaped."
Past Perfect Continuous Subject + had been + Verb-ing An ongoing past action that continued up until another specific past point. "The hacker had been downloading classified files for two hours before we cut the link."
Present Simple Subject + Verb 1 (-s/-es) Universal truths, operating procedures, habits, or scheduled routines. "The night guard patrolls the vault every hour on the dot."
Present Continuous Subject + is/am/are + Verb-ing Live operations or actions happening right now at this exact moment. "The forensic unit is analyzing the red fibers found on the window."
Present Perfect Subject + have/has + Verb 3 Past actions with undeniable relevance, evidence, or impact on the present. "The cleaner has wiped the prints; the glass is completely clear."
Present Perfect Continuous Subject + have/has been + Verb-ing An action that started in the past and is still actively continuing right now. "The team have been tracking this specific cryptocurrency wallet since dawn."
Future Simple Subject + will + Verb 1 Predictions, official promises, or instant decisions regarding the investigation. "The cyber unit will trace the IP address before sunrise."
Future Continuous Subject + will be + Verb-ing An action that will be actively in progress at a specific time in the future. "At midnight, the tactical team will be waiting inside the warehouse."
Future Perfect Subject + will have + Verb 3 An objective that will be completely finished before a future deadline. "By tomorrow noon, the smuggler will have sold the stolen artifact."
Future Perfect Continuous Subject + will have been + Verb-ing Continuous action measured or counted up to a specific milestone in the future. "By next Friday, the lead inspector will have been running this case for a year."
